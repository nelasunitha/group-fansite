Fun Facts

About Biryani:

Biryani is a fragrant rice dish layered with marinated meat or vegetables, seasoned with aromatic spices, and cooked to perfection. Originating from Persia, it has become a beloved dish across India with regional variations like Hyderabadi, Lucknowi, and Kolkata biryani.

Types of Biryani

Experience diverse flavors, from the spicy Hyderabadi biryani to the mild Lucknowi version, or the unique flavors of Malabar and Kolkata biryanis.
  • Vegetarian Biryani: A vibrant and flavorful dish of fragrant basmati rice cooked with a medley of fresh, colorful vegetables such as carrots, beans, peas, and bell peppers. It’s infused with a symphony of aromatic spices like cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, and bay leaves, creating a mouthwatering aroma. The biryani is elevated with layers of caramelized onions, golden roasted cashews, and raisins for a hint of sweetness. A touch of saffron and ghee adds richness, while fresh coriander and mint leaves provide a refreshing finish. Served with raita or a tangy pickle, this biryani is a feast for both the eyes and the palate.
  • Non-Vegetarian Biryani: A rich and hearty dish featuring fragrant basmati rice layered with tender, succulent pieces of marinated chicken, mutton, or shrimp, cooked to perfection. The meat is slow-cooked with a blend of bold spices like cumin, coriander, cinnamon, and star anise, along with saffron-infused rice for a royal touch. Each layer is enriched with ghee, fried onions, and fresh herbs, creating a symphony of flavors. Garnished with crispy onions, boiled eggs, and a squeeze of lemon, this biryani is traditionally served with cooling raita (yogurt raita) or spicy salan, making it a complete and indulgent meal.
